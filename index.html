Petroleum

A petroleum car is a vehicle that uses petroleum as its fuel. Petroleum cars typically use a internal combustion engine. The air/fuel mixture is ignited in the combustion chamber, which causes the piston to move down and create power.

Electric

An electric car is a vehicle that is propelled by one or more electric motors, using only energy stored in batteries. Electric cars are becoming increasingly popular as they offer a number of advantages over traditional gasoline-powered cars

Hybrid

A hybrid car is a vehicle that combines an internal combustion engine (ICE) with an electric motor. The ICE and electric motor work together to power the car, which can improve fuel efficiency and reduce emissions.

Top Cars

Lamborghini Huracan STO

The Lamborghini Huracan STO is a track-focused version of the Huracan that is powered by a 5.2-liter naturally aspirated V10 engine that produces 640 horsepower and 413 pound-feet of torque. It can go from 0 to 60 mph in 3.0 seconds and has a top speed of 202 mph.

Bugatti Chiron Super Sport 300+

This is the fastest production car in the world, with a top speed of 304.773 mph. It is powered by a quad-turbocharged 8.0-liter W16 engine that produces 1,600 horsepower and 1,180 lb-ft of torque.

Ferrari F8 Tributo

This is a plug-in hybrid sports car that is powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine and three electric motors that produce a combined 986 horsepower and 590 pound-feet of torque. It can go from 0 to 60 mph in 2.5 seconds and has a top speed of 211 mph.
